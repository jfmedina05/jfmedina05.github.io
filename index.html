About

Engineer.
Leader.
Builder.

Jaiden Medina is a Computer Engineering student at Indiana University Bloomington's Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering, expected to graduate in 2027. He has accepted an Accelerated 4+1 M.S. offer in Intelligent Systems Engineering with a Computer Engineering specialization, beginning Fall 2026.

His technical work spans embedded systems, edge AI, digital logic design, data engineering, hardware reliability, robotics, and manufacturing optimization — each grounded in a core belief that engineering should create real, human-centered impact.

His long-term interest is in healthcare, medical device technology, and human-centered systems — building engineering solutions that improve daily life through reliable hardware, intelligent software, and data-driven design.

He pairs that technical foundation with deep organizational leadership through SHPE, Tau Epsilon Kappa, and the Luddy School Student Ambassador program — building community, mentoring peers, and opening doors for underrepresented students in STEM.

Academic Project · Microelectronics
↗
Radiation-Induced Fault Characterization in Sequential Logic
Investigated how radiation-induced charge disturbances affect the reliability of sequential CMOS logic by modeling transient current injections in a 4-bit master-slave D flip-flop shift register. Used LTspice simulations to analyze single-event transients, single-event upsets, node-level sensitivity, clock-dependent vulnerability, and master versus slave latch susceptibility.
